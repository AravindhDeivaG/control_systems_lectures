Cauchy’s argument principle:

  • Let’s consider a simple transfer function G(S)=S+1G(S) = S+1G(S)=S+1﻿.
  • Pick a point on S plane and substitute it in the transfer function.
  • The new point so obtained is plotted on W plane.
  • Repeat this process for all the points along a closed contour.

  • If the S plane contour encloses a zero, then the contour mapped to the W plane encloses origin.
  • Also the direction of tracing (CW / CCW) remains the same.

  • Similarly If the S plane contour encloses a pole, then the contour mapped to the W plane encloses origin, but the direction of tracing (CW / CCW) is reversed.

Pole on imaginary axis:

  • If there is a pole on the imaginary axis
    • The gain tends to infinity
    • Discontinuous jump in phase
  • Hence the Nyquist contour is slightly bent around the pole.
  • Although the gain at that point still remains infinity the change in phase is smooth unlike the abrupt change when the contour passes through the pole.
